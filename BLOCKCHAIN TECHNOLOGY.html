BLOCKCHAIN TECHNOLOGY

  • January 25, 2019January 25, 2019
  • by Mrudula Bapat

Blockchain, the foundation of Bitcoin, has received a lot of hype recently. Blockchain serves as an immutable ledger which allows transactions to take place in a decentralized manner. This technology is one of the newest technologies and is supposed to have a great impact on many future applications.

What is Blockchain?

The basic idea of blockchain is that it is a distributed database that maintains a continuously growing list of records. These list of records called as blocks are linked using cryptography. Each block contains a cryptographic hash of the previous block, a timestamp, and transaction data. By design, a blockchain is resistant to modification of the data. It is an open, distributed ledger that can record transactions between two parties efficiently and in a verifiable and permanent way.

The blockchain is an ingenious invention of a person or group of people known by the pseudonym, Satoshi Nakamoto in 2008 to serve as the public transaction ledger of the cryptocurrency bitcoin. The invention of blockchain for bitcoin made so that transactions can be made without the need for a trusted authority or a third party. The Blockchain technology which was initially created for digital currency has found other applications too.

How does Blockchain work?

Consider the analogy of a word document, the traditional way of sending information is to send a Word document to a particular person and then the person would reply back by making changes to that document. The problem with this scenario is that you have to wait until the other person replies back or sends the document back with the changes made. Until that time you cannot make changes to the document that is two people cannot work with the same document at once. With Google Docs or Google Sheets, two or more people can make changes or view changes made to the document.

Blockchain technology works in a similar way and allows a shared ledger so that multiple people can view it. It is also not owned by a single person or entity instead it is distributed and hence decentralized. Blockchain is also immutable and so no one can tamper the data inside the Blockchain. It gives transparency of transactions as well as gives privacy to the user, that is all the transactions made can be viewed easily but the userâ€™s real identity is secure as only the public address of the user is visible.

Applications of Blockchain:

Many new businesses will be using Blockchain because of its distributed ledger technology which would make their transactions more transparent and trustworthy.

Some of the applications are:

Smart contracts

The distributed ledgers enable the coding of simple contracts that will execute when specified conditions are met. These smart contracts can be used in the real estate industry to make dealings involving several parties and individuals.

Governance

Using this technology we can make the results fully transparent to the public and accessible. It can bring complete transparency in the election process.

Crowdfunding

People nowadays want to have a direct say in the product development With the help of Blockchain we can create a crowd-sourced venture capital funds.

Supply chain auditing

Companies make many claims regarding the quality and authenticity of their product and people need to know whether these claims are true or not. With distributed ledgers we can check whether the claims of the things we buy are genuine or not.

File storage

Sending information in a decentralized way has many benefits. Two or more people can edit the same document at the same time also the changes made can be timely and easily verified.
