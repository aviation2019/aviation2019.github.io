Dear Parents

  • February 27, 2019
  • by Bhagyashree Sudhir Sakharkar
  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Google+

  • Tumblr

Dear Parents,

You might be surprised to find this letter as we are always together but I think sometimes you fail to see how much hollow I feel about my self. I feel like I am a big failure and I can’t do anything about it but I swear I tried hard, I tried really hard to make you feel proud about me but every time I did something that I thought would make you happy to have a child like me you said that “Well that was good, but you could do better”
Can I please once hear “Well done child, i am proud of you” Is it really hard to make you happy? or its just that I am not good enough at anything? I know i have not done something exceptionally good in studies nor I am any good in sports or extra curriculum activities but isn’t it appreciable that I tried? Is it really necessary every time to criticise every time? Yes,You might say we do that so you’ll do more better next. But no I don’t feel motivated after you say that I feel like I let you down.
All I want is you appreciating me for what I am even if I am average at what I am doing that make be will motivate me and try putting more effort in my work next time. I want you to be happy for me even if I am not best among all others. I want you to tell me “Dear, We are happy you tried and we believe you’ll achieve want you want to. We are proud of you kid”
Is it too much I am asking for?
Your’s ever,
Mediocre child
